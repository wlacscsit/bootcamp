LA High Tech Bridge & Coding Boot Camp

The Bridge to Coding training is comprised of a two-week session where students are prepared with contextualized information to enter the Coding Boot Camp. Students will be exposed to important aspects of being a new employee that includes: Workplace Success, Shining as a New Employee, Oral and Written Communication for the IT office environment, Workplace Behavior, Emotional Intelligence, Cultural Diversity, and Interviewing Skills. The 16 week Coding Boot Camp is designed to prepare 135 programmers and coders to transition into job openings in the Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) industry. Training in the LA High Tech Bridge & Coding Boot Camp (HTBC2) includes an 8-week (2 days per week) paid internship.

Coding Skills

Students completing the Coding BootCamp will be able to:

  • Develop web applications using a web based full stack programming environment
  • Design, implement and test web applications
  • Design web user interfaces using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • Use web services to transfer data and add interactive components to web pages

Informational and Assessment Session

Students who are interested in enrolling are invited to attend informational and assessment sessions held on December 3rd and December 10th, 2016 in room CE 222 at 2pm. To be eligible to enroll in the boot camp, students must be 18 years or older, have completed college level English and Math, and basic computer literacy. For students without prior programming knowledge or experience, it is recommended you enroll in CS 902 during the Winter session, and continue to CS 962 and CS 957 in the Spring semester parallel to the boot camp.

Please bring an unofficial copy of your transcripts and be prepared to take a small assessment during the session.
